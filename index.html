History

What Are Nito Vines, and Why Do They Cling to Everything?

Nito vines (Lygodium circinatum) are a type of climbing fern found in the Philippines, particularly in the southern islands of Mindanao, Mindoro, and Panay. Known for their natural strength and flexibility, these vines are crafted into beautiful and durable items like baskets, trays, and tablemats. The vines' strength and flexibility come from their structure, which allows artisans to weave them into detailed and durable products. This craft has been passed down through generations and is an important part of Filipino culture.

Nito vines cling to everything they can as they grow. They wrap around trees, rocks, and other plants to reach sunlight, which helps them thrive. This clinging behavior makes it easier for people to collect the vines for crafting.

Overall, nito vines are important in the Philippines for both their natural and cultural value. They support local economies and help preserve traditional craftsmanship through sustainable, eco-friendly products.

How Are Nito Vines Woven?

The weaving process is as intricate as the products themselves:

  • Technique: Artisans use a circular weaving motion to create distinctive patterns.
  • Versatility: The natural flexibility of nito allows for detailed designs without compromising strength.

Example: A weaver might spend days crafting a single basket, ensuring every detail is flawless.

Procedure:

  • Gathering: First, the nito vines are gathered from the forest, where they typically grow on trees and plants.
  • Drying: The gathered vines are then exposed to sunlight to dry them out. This process makes the vines more pliable and easier to work with.
  • Preparing: Once dried, the outer layer of the vine is removed to get to the inner core, which is used for weaving. This core is about the thickness of a pencil.
  • Weaving: The prepared strips of nito vines are then woven into the desired shape, such as baskets or other handicrafts.
  • Finishing: After weaving, the finished product is often varnished with a protective coating and left to dry again before it's ready to use.
